## ‚òï COFFEE & HOT DRINKS

### Setup (3 minutes)
**Location:** Near window, not in direct sunlight  
**Surface:** Clean counter, wood table, or white backdrop  
**Time:** 10am-3pm (natural light, not harsh)

**What You Need:**
- Freshly made drink
- Clean cup (no drips or spills)
- Simple backdrop (plain wall, wood, white)
- Optional: Saucer, spoon, small prop (sugar packet)

---

### üì∏ Shot Technique

#### Overhead Shot (Top-Down)
**Best for:** Cappuccinos, lattes with foam art

**Steps:**
1. Place drink on clean surface near window
2. Stand directly above drink
3. Hold iPhone **horizontally** (landscape)
4. Tap on drink to focus
5. Slide brightness UP (+1 to +2)
6. Take 5-10 shots (move slightly for best angle)

**Composition:**
- Center the cup in frame
- Leave space around edges (negative space)
- Capture foam art if present
- Include saucer or small prop

---

#### 45¬∞ Angle Shot (Classic)
**Best for:** All drinks, shows depth and steam**

**Steps:**
1. Position drink at eye level
2. Stand 2-3 feet away
3. Shoot from 45¬∞ angle (not straight-on)
4. Hold iPhone **horizontally**
5. Tap to focus on cup
6. Take 5-10 shots

**Composition:**
- Cup slightly off-center (rule of thirds)
- Show handle (adds dimension)
- Background slightly blurred (tap on cup to focus)
- Natural light from side (window)

---

### Styling Tips for Hot Drinks:
- **Steam is Gold:** Shoot immediately after making (capture steam)
- **Clean Rims:** Wipe any drips/spills
- **Logo Forward:** Show cup branding if you have it
- **Props:** Spoon, cookie, sugar packet (don't overcrowd)
- **Background:** Blur it (tap on cup to focus)

## üßä COLD DRINKS & ICED BEVERAGES

### Setup
**Location:** Same as hot drinks (window light)  
**Key Difference:** Condensation is your friend (shows freshness)

---

### üì∏ Shot Technique

#### Straight-On Shot
**Best for:** Tall iced drinks, smoothies, frappes

**Steps:**
1. Place drink on clean surface
2. Stand 2-3 feet away at drink's eye level
3. Hold iPhone **vertically** (portrait)
4. Center the drink
5. Tap to focus on glass
6. Take 5-10 shots

**Composition:**
- Center the drink vertically
- Show full height of glass
- Include straw (adds color)
- Capture condensation (freshness indicator)
- Background simple/clean

---

### Styling Tips for Cold Drinks:
- **Fresh is Best:** Shoot within 1-2 minutes of making
- **Condensation:** Shows drink is cold and fresh
- **Straw Positioning:** Angle it nicely, not straight up
- **Ice Visible:** Show ice through glass (clarity)
- **Garnishes:** Mint, fruit, whipped cream on top
- **Colorful Drinks:** Adjust brightness to make colors pop

## ü•ê PASTRIES & BAKED GOODS

### Setup
**Location:** Near window, natural light from 45¬∞ angle  
**Surface:** Wood cutting board, white plate, clean counter  
**Avoid:** Overhead harsh lighting (makes shadows)

---

### üì∏ Shot Technique

#### Close-Up Shot
**Best for:** Muffins, croissants, cookies, donuts

**Steps:**
1. Place item on plate/board
2. Position near window (side lighting)
3. Get close (1-2 feet away)
4. Hold iPhone **horizontally**
5. Tap to focus on product
6. Slightly angle camera (not straight-on)
7. Take 10+ shots (move around it)

**Composition:**
- Fill frame with pastry (get close)
- Show texture (flaky layers, crumb)
- Angle it slightly (shows dimension)
- Use rule of thirds (slightly off-center)
- Include crumbs for realism

---

### üì∏ Multiple Items
**Best for:** Variety display, cookie selection

**Steps:**
1. Arrange 3-5 items on board/plate
2. Vary heights (stack some, lean others)
3. Overhead or 45¬∞ angle
4. Don't overcrowd
5. Leave negative space

---

### Styling Tips for Pastries:
- **Fresh Out of Oven:** Best time to shoot (warm = inviting)
- **Break One Open:** Show inside (muffin, croissant)
- **Texture Matters:** Zoom in on flaky layers, crumbs
- **Props:** Coffee cup in background (tells story)
- **Plate/Board:** Wood or white (don't use busy patterns)
- **Crumbs are OK:** Shows authenticity

### üì∏ Shot Technique

## üé® LIGHTING GUIDE

### Natural Light (BEST)
**Window Light Setup:**
- Place item 2-3 feet from window
- Light should come from **side** (45¬∞ angle)
- Avoid direct sunlight (too harsh)
- Best time: 10am-3pm
- Cloudy day = soft, even light (actually great!)

### What to Avoid:
- ‚ùå Overhead fluorescent lights (yellow cast, harsh shadows)
- ‚ùå Direct sunlight (too bright, overexposed)
- ‚ùå Evening/night (need too much artificial light)
- ‚ùå Flash (washes out colors, looks unnatural)

## ‚úèÔ∏è EDITING (2 minutes per photo)

### iPhone Photos App

1. **Open photo** ‚Üí Tap "Edit"
2. **Auto Enhance** (try it first)
3. **Manual Adjustments:**
   - **Light:** +10 to +25 (brighten)
   - **Contrast:** +5 to +15 (make it pop)
   - **Saturation:** +5 to +10 (food should look vibrant)
   - **Warmth:** +5 (makes coffee look warmer)
   - **Sharpness:** +10 to +15 (details pop)
4. **Crop:** Remove distractions, center product
5. **Straighten:** Use level tool if needed
6. **Done** ‚Üí Save

### Food Photography Editing Tips:
- **Warm is Better:** Slightly warm tones (+5 warmth) make food inviting
- **Bright is Key:** Food photos should be bright and airy
- **Don't Overdo Saturation:** +5 to +10 max (looks fake otherwise)
- **Sharpen Details:** Especially for pastries (texture is important)

## üö´ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Too Dark** ‚Üí Shoot near window + boost brightness in edit
2. **Messy Background** ‚Üí Use plain surface (white/wood)
3. **Food Looks Cold** ‚Üí Shoot immediately after preparation
4. **Flash Used** ‚Üí NEVER use flash for food
5. **Too Much in Frame** ‚Üí Get closer, fill frame with product
6. **Not Sharp** ‚Üí Tap to focus before every shot
7. **Overhead Lights** ‚Üí Use window light instead
8. **Yellow Cast** ‚Üí Adjust "Warmth" slider in editing
9. **Glare on Cups** ‚Üí Angle light differently
10. **Rushed Setup** ‚Üí Take time to clean, arrange, style

---

## üí° PRO TIPS

1. **Shoot Everything:** Make full menu item, take 10+ photos, pick best
2. **Steam = Freshness:** Capture steam on hot drinks
3. **Condensation = Cold:** Don't wipe it off cold drinks
4. **Break It Open:** Show inside of muffin, croissant
5. **Tell a Story:** Include props (spoon, napkin, but keep minimal)
6. **Natural is Best:** Don't over-style, keep it simple
7. **Consistency:** Use same setup for all photos (looks professional)
8. **Shoot Horizontal:** Most menu photos look better landscape
9. **Get Low:** Eye-level shots are more engaging than looking down
10. **Edit Lightly:** Keep colors natural, don't over-saturate
