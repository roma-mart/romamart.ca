# Menu Photography Guide

> Guidelines for RoCafé menu item photography

## Overview

Appetizing menu photography drives customer interest and sales. This guide covers best practices for photographing RoCafé menu items.

## Equipment

### Recommended

- Camera with macro capability
- 50mm or 85mm lens
- Diffused lighting setup
- Light reflector
- Clean backdrop

### Minimal Setup

- Modern smartphone
- Window with natural light
- White foam board reflector

## Shooting Guidelines

### Composition

1. **Angle:** 45-degree angle most versatile
2. **Fill Frame:** Item should fill 70-80% of frame
3. **Background:** Clean, uncluttered
4. **Props:** Minimal, brand-consistent

### Lighting

- Soft, diffused natural light preferred
- Avoid harsh shadows
- Side lighting adds dimension
- Use reflector to fill shadows

### Food Styling

- Prepare fresh items
- Steam/heat for authenticity
- Garnish appropriately
- Clean plate edges

## Shot Types

| Type | Angle | Use Case |
|------|-------|----------|
| Hero | 45° | Menu highlights |
| Flat Lay | 90° | Combo shots |
| Side | 0° | Layered items |
| Detail | Macro | Texture shots |

## Technical Settings

### Camera

- Aperture: f/2.8-f/5.6 (shallow depth)
- ISO: 100-400
- White balance: Match lighting

### Post-Processing

- Color correction for accuracy
- Exposure adjustment
- Slight contrast boost
- No heavy filtering

## Item Categories

### Hot Beverages

- Show steam (natural or added)
- Clean cup/mug
- Latte art if applicable
- Brand-consistent cups

### Cold Beverages

- Condensation is appealing
- Clear glass shows color
- Ice should look fresh
- Garnishes fresh

### Food Items

- Hot items: Show steam
- Sandwiches: Show layers
- Pastries: Show texture
- Portion size realistic

## File Deliverables

| Use | Format | Dimensions |

## Brand Consistency

- Use brand colors in props/backgrounds
- Consistent lighting style
- Same editing treatment
- Navy/yellow accent items
